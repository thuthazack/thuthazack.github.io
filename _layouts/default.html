A little bit about me...

Welcome! My name is Thuta Aung, but you can call me Zack. I am a fresh graduate from University of Michigan with a BSE in computer engineering. My primary area of focus is within firmware/low level software development of low powered embedded systems.

I like to keep myself busy at all times. Currently, I am working on developing a device called Smart Sensor Interface as a part of Professor Robert Dick's team while working as a part time marketing manager of a startup that I cofounded called BridgeBurma.

My past works...

Smart Sensor Interface

This project describes an Internet-of-Things sensor interface that converts bare sensors and manual sensing systems into automated, wireless sensing systems. It has the goal of supporting a very wide range of sensing applications, implying the need for a very general design. The sensing system has several novel design features dictated by the application requirements including automated calibration to compensate for process variation and temperature dependence of components within the sensing system.

Key skills used: ANSI-C, C++, Python, MATLAB, Eagle, PCB prototyping, Cellular Communication, UART, SPI, I2C, Logic Analyzer, FreeRTOS, Atmel Studios.

Spinosaurus

The proposed goal of our wearable Spinosaurus was to provide weightlifters feedback on their back form while squatting and deadlifting. It is comprised of a vest (the SpineAligner) that stretches across the spin and an Android phone app. The vest has the ability to measure the orientation of the user's back with three inertial measurment units (IMUs) and then communicates with the phone app to display visual feedback.

Key skills used: PCB designing, debugging and validation, BlueTooth, Android App, SPI, UART, Eagle, and Arduino.

BridgeBurma

Aiming to mitigate the effects of braindrain in Myanmar, BridgeBurma was founded by four passionate college students in 2018. Gaining an audience of over a thousand individuals within little over a year, we aim to share collective wisdom between Burmese individuals all across the world.

Our primary service is an online forum with side features such as direct chatting with our mentors/scholars.

Within our community, we strongly promote self-development and firmly believes that everyone deserves an equal opportunity at pursuing higher education regardless of their backgrounds.
