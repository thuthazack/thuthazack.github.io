My past works...

Smart Sensor Interface

This article describes an Internet-of-Things sensor interface that converts bare sensors and manual sensing systems into automated, wireless sensing systems. It has the goal of supporting a very wide range of sensing applications, implying the need for a very general design. The sensing system has several novel design features dictated by the application requirements including automated calibration to compensate for process variation and temperature dependence of components within the sensing system.

Key skills used: ANSI-C, C++, Python, MATLAB, Eagle, PCB prototyping, Cellular Communication, UART, SPI, I2C, Logic Analyzer, FreeRTOS.

Spinosaurus

The proposed goal of our wearable Spinosaurus was to provide weightlifters feedback on their back form while squatting and deadlifting. It is comprised of a vest (the SpineAligner) that stretches across the spin and an Android phone app. The vest has the ability to measure the orientation of the user's back with three inertial measurment units (IMUs) and then communicates with the phone app to display visual feedback.

Key skills used: PCB designing, debugging and validation, SPI, UART, Eagle, and Arduino.

BridgeBurma

Aiming to mitigate the effects of braindrain in Myanmar, BridgeBurma was founded by four passionate college students in 2018. Gaining an audience of over a thousand individuals within little over a year, we aim to share collective wisdom between Burmese individuals all across the world.

Our primary service is an online forum with side features such as direct chatting with our mentors/scholars.

Within our community, we strongly promote self-development and firmly believes that everyone deserves an equal opportunity at pursuing higher education regardless of their backgrounds.
